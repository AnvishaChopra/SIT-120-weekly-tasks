A STORY IN MY WORDS...

This all started in 2021 when a random idea of an India girl to pursue her higher studies in Australia took over her. With absolutely no clue what to do next she started searching for Youtube videos to find her way around. The only thing that she was sure about was the support from her parents. They were there for her, come what may!

Which university? Which course? Can she get a 100% scholarship?


She searched for days, followed by weeks. But it simply seemed like a never ending task. But she was not gonna give up that easily.By the end of month two she got her answer. It was gonna be none other than Deakin University.

Wohhhooooooooo she did her victoy dance. Time for task two now. Which course? That wasn't so tough for her to decide. Wanna know why??? Read her views below on her vision, execution plan, which path she wanted to take to complete her aim.


For people without special needs Artificial intelligence and integrated technologies can make things easier but for people with such special needs it can make things possible. Her vision was to contribute to the field of special education for people with special needs with the help of AI and integrated concepts. She had always believed that such people are like beautiful butterflies but with broken wings and need some extra help to spread them again. With the knowledge of such modern technologies, She as an individual can prove to be an asset that would be able to provide help so that they are able spread their wings once more. Furthermore, She intended to expand her realm and be a help to other communities, being a help to humanity at large.
By applying for the Bachelors of information Technology course at Deakin University She would get an edge over the others by being a part of a work integrating environment along with the chance to collaborate with like-minded peers and work on projects, which would not only strengthen my theoretical concepts but also give a life to it my putting it to practical use. According to her research one of the plus points of the University is also that it has a Disability resource center (DRC) with its disability, access and inclusion plan. In her capacity as a Deakin student she plans to engage with this faculty and use it as an idea generator for my projects related to my vision. This way she would be able to integrate the concepts that are being taught at the university and put it to practical use. This would help her to work on her vision and not simply wait for her degree to get completed to start the execution of her future plans.I think you got your answer now! :) I know she is amazing right!!
